Introduction:

We are a team from the Teachers College Deaf and Hard of Hearing Program, Class of 2025—joined by three international students from China. This website aims to showcase a comparison of Deaf cultures in the U.S. (≈11 million Deaf) and China (≈27 million Deaf), foster cross-cultural exchange from the 1880 Milan Conference to today’s community archives, and advocate for digital inclusion through real-time captions, sign-language interpretation, and customizable interfaces so that Deaf users and others with special needs can fully engage online.

Why Our Work Is Important?

By comparing Deaf cultures in the U.S. and China, we shine a light on both shared experiences and unique traditions that often go unnoticed. Documenting community archives preserves this history for future generations, while advocating for social-media accessibility—like real-time captions, sign-language interpretation, and customizable interfaces—helps ensure Deaf users and others with special needs can join conversations online.

Together, these efforts promote a more inclusive digital world and strengthen connections across borders.
