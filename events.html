Community Reflections

Thoughts from our Deaf Asian Event participants

Ella

"This was my first time attending a Deaf Asian event, and it was such a wonderful experience! I met so many amazing, friendly people who welcomed me wholeheartedly. Even though I’ve only been learning ASL for a year, everyone was patient—waiting as I stumbled through signs, offering praise and helpful feedback, and lending a hand whenever I needed it. I could really feel the strong sense of unity in the Deaf community and see how each person celebrates their culture and identity in their own way. I can’t wait to join again next time!"

Community ASL Learning

Tina

Attending the Asian Street Festival was such a joyful and meaningful experience! I had the chance to represent Teachers College, Columbia University to introduce the TC Deaf Education program, connect with Deaf and hearing people from diverse backgrounds, and share stories about Deaf culture in both America and China. It was especially inspiring to see so many people—Deaf and hearing—coming together, showing curiosity and support for sign language and identity. Moments like this remind me how powerful community, language, and visibility truly are. I'm so grateful to be part of it.
